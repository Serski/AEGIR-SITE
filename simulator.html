Aegir Battle Simulator — Compact Summary

Configure opposing task forces, track shield and morale shifts, and resolve three-round engagements in Aegir’s strategic sandbox. The following outline captures the current rules draft.

1. Match Setup

Dual fleet panels let you assign ship counts by class, choose a commander, pick one formation, one tactic, per-class orders for each round, and toggle withdrawal attempts. Battles resolve across three rounds covering Detection & Initiative, Engagement, and Resolution, with optional withdraw checks after rounds one and two (slower fleets risk pursuit).

2. Ship Roster, Base Stats & Shields

Each hull lists Attack / Defense / Hull Points / Speed / Shield level (capacity • regen). The roster currently includes:

  • Corvette — 45 / 25 / 300 / 90 / L0 (no shields).
  • Destroyer — 70 / 55 / 550 / 65 / L1 (200 • 10).
  • Heavy Frigate — 90 / 75 / 800 / 45 / L2 (300 • 15).
  • Cruiser — 130 / 90 / 1200 / 35 / L3 (500 • 20).
  • Battleship — 190 / 130 / 2000 / 20 / L4 (800 • 25).
  • Mega Battle Station — 1300 / 900 / 12000 / 0 / L5 (5000 • 50).

Starting with all six classes unlocks the Combined-Arms bonus: +10% Command Sync, –10% incoming damage, and one Volley Coordination declaration (+15% fleet damage) during Detection.

3. Formations

Pick one fleet-wide stance:

  • Spearhead — increased attack, reduced evasion.
  • Phalanx — boosted defense and shield regeneration, lower initiative.
  • Dispersed Line — heightened evasion, reduced accuracy.

4. Tactics

Choose one approach lasting all three rounds:

  • Aggressive — +15% damage (+25% in round one), +5% crit, +10% incoming damage, and –5% shield regen.
  • Balanced — +5% accuracy, +5% damage, +8 morale at the end of each round, and –5% incoming damage.
  • Defensive — –15% damage, +30% shield regen and +10% shield capacity (non-Corvettes), +5% evasion for Corvettes and Destroyers, and +10% withdraw success after rounds one and two.

5. Class Special Orders

Assign one order per class each round. Highlights include:

  • Corvettes — Flank (extra accuracy and crit vs capitals, more damage taken), Evade Pattern (high evasion, lower damage, safer in belts), or Overdrive (faster initiative, more damage, accuracy drop, minor self-hull tick).
  • Destroyers — Shield Screen (redirect hits, raise shield cap), Target Lock (accuracy and crit vs DD/Frig, ignore evasion), or PD Overclock (intercept boost, lower damage and regen).
  • Heavy Frigates — Suppressive Volley (enemy accuracy/evasion debuff next round, self damage penalty) or Focus Fire (big bonus vs one class, heavy penalty elsewhere).
  • Cruisers — Broadside (large damage spike, accuracy loss vs evasive, cooldown), Lance Overcharge (shield pierce vs capitals, temporary shield penalty, misfire risk), or Command Relay (fleet command and morale boost, damage trade-off).
  • Battleships — Spinal Salvo (burst damage with cooldown), Fortress Posture (armor & PD boost, initiative and damage penalty), Command Beacon (fleet accuracy/crit and escort evasion, reduced battleship damage), or Shock Barrage (enemy accuracy and shield regen debuff).
  • Mega Battle Station — Bastion Shield (fleet regen/cap boost, station heavies offline), Cataclysm Lance (shield-piercing strike with shockwave, regen penalty, max two uses), Grav-Well Snare (locks withdraw, hinders enemy initiative/evasion), or Dock & Repair (restore allied capital hull/shields, disables station PD this round).

Effects stack in the order: Formation → Tactic → Class Orders → Theater/Event.

6. Battle Theaters

Pick one environment to shape engagements:

  • Gas-Giant Storm Layer — Lightning storms jam comms: lower beam accuracy, higher shield regen, random sensor desyncs.
  • Asteroid Belts — Small craft gain evasion, capital ships lose initiative, collision checks trigger each round.
  • Station Yard / High Orbit — Friendly turrets assist, better salvage on victory, with political fallout risk for wide-area fire.
  • Graveglass Veil / Nebula — Harder detection, beam attenuation, and ghost-lock misfires.
  • Temporal-Slip Corridor — Scrambled initiative, crit re-rolls, and dangerous retreats.

7. Round Flow & Outcomes

Each round resolves in three phases:

  1. Detection & Initiative — Formation, speed, and ECM (notably in the Graveglass Veil) establish firing order.
  2. Engagement — Apply tactics and orders, deal damage, track shields, criticals, and morale.
  3. Resolution — Tally disabled or routed units, adjust morale/command, and trigger events.

After three rounds, report outcomes as Victory, Pyrrhic Victory, Stalemate, Collapse, or Anomaly (sector oddities). Loot, salvage, commander experience, repairs, and theater fallout follow.

Core raid concepts mirror the bot’s combat logic, blending weighted stats, variance, and tiered results.
